# Music Streaming Customer Churn Analysis (EDA)

This project focuses on performing Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a 3-month music streaming dataset to understand user behavior and identify factors contributing to customer churn. The analysis involves data cleaning, preprocessing, visualization, and trend analysis to uncover patterns in customer engagement, listening habits, subscription activity, and retention.

## Objectives

* Analyze customer behavior over a 3-month period.
* Identify key indicators associated with customer churn.
* Explore listening patterns, session activity, and user engagement metrics.
* Generate actionable insights to support customer retention strategies.

## Tools & Technologies

* Python
* Pandas
* NumPy
* Seaborn
* Jupyter Notebook

## Key Analysis Performed

* Data Cleaning and Preprocessing
* Missing Value Treatment
* Customer Activity Analysis
* Listening Time and Session Analysis
* Feature Distribution and Correlation Analysis
* Data Visualization and Insight Generation

## Outcome


* Customers receiving higher discounts were more likely to cancel their subscriptions, suggesting that discounts alone may not be sufficient to improve long-term retention.
* Users with higher listening hours showed lower churn rates, indicating that engagement is a strong predictor of customer retention.
* Customers who spent less time listening to music were more likely to cancel their subscriptions.
* Users with a higher preference for Pop music exhibited a greater likelihood of churn compared to listeners of other genres.
* Customer engagement metrics, particularly listening hours and session activity, were among the strongest indicators of subscription retention.
* The analysis highlights the importance of increasing user engagement and understanding genre-specific behavior to reduce customer churn.
